# Competitor Research — Widget Customization Cycle

## Sources checked
- Widgetsmith site/App Store/web writeups
- Widgy App Store/web writeups
- Color Widgets site/App Store/web writeups
- WidgetClub site/App Store/web writeups
- Student planner app positioning: MyStudyLife and planner roundup references

## Widgetsmith
**What works**
- Clear promise: make the phone yours.
- Starts from widget sizes and content types, not a blank settings list.
- Strong mental model: pick widget → customize appearance → install.
- The product sells identity and utility together.

**Adapt for StudyPlanner**
- Make Widget Studio a flagship tab.
- Lead with real planner data previews.
- Explain free native widgets vs Plus advanced customization honestly.

## Widgy
**What works**
- Power-user customization and importable templates.
- Photoshop-like flexibility, but can become complex.
- Template-first path matters because blank-canvas creation is too much for most users.

**Adapt for StudyPlanner**
- Avoid full blank-canvas editor for v1.
- Use constrained templates: Today, Upcoming, Week, Class Risk, Focus Block.
- Make every control visible in preview immediately.

## Color Widgets
**What works**
- Gallery-first discovery.
- Themes combine widgets, icons, wallpapers into a coherent aesthetic.
- Fast path to value: browse → select → customize.

**Adapt for StudyPlanner**
- Theme packs should restyle app + widget together.
- Treat customization as a visual system, not isolated toggles.

## WidgetClub
**What works**
- Massive searchable template inventory.
- Bundled 3-in-1 themes reduce decision fatigue.
- Beginner-friendly “make it look good now” positioning.

**Adapt for StudyPlanner**
- Recommended widgets based on student state.
- Template cards should say when/why to use them.

## Student planner apps
**What works**
- Clear data model: classes, homework, exams, reminders.
- Best products explain what to do next, not just show calendars.

**Adapt for StudyPlanner**
- 60-second model: Scan/Add → Review → Today/Week → Widgets/Reminders.
- Keep Scan and Review central; do not bury the syllabus promise under customization.
